Sport / track Sub-track Rationale — what strength gap does this fix?
CrossFit 4-week cycles · 2–3 days/week
CrossFit Lower body strength
Squat patterns, posterior chain
Back squat, front squat, Romanian DL, hip thrust
 Most CF athletes are quad-dominant with weak glutes and hamstrings. Fixing this improves clean, thruster, wall ball, box jump, and all squat-based movements. The highest-leverage sub-track for most CF athletes.
Overhead & shoulder strength
Press, stability, scapular control
Strict press, push press, Z-press, DB press, banded pull-aparts
 Shoulder weakness and instability is the #1 limiter for gymnastics (HSPU, ring work, muscle-ups) and overhead barbell work (snatch, jerk). This track builds the pressing and stabilizing strength gymnastic volume can't provide.
Pulling strength
Horizontal and vertical pulling
Weighted pull-up, bent-over row, single-arm DB row, face pull
 CF athletes do enormous pressing volume (push-ups, dips, HSPU) but almost no loaded pulling outside kipping. This creates shoulder imbalances and limits muscle-up strength. Direct pulling work fixes the imbalance and boosts pulling gymnastics.
Muscular endurance
Moderate load, higher rep ranges
Tempo squats, DB circuits, kettlebell complexes, barbell cycling
 For athletes who can move well but fade in longer metcons. Programs moderate-load, moderate-rep work with short rest — trains the ability to move weight repeatedly without breakdown. Improves barbell cycling and touch-and-go efficiency.
Hyrox 5-week cycles · 2–3 days/week
Hyrox Sled & loaded carry strength
Hip drive, unilateral, core bracing
Safety bar squat, Bulgarian split squat, farmer carry, sled work
 The sled push/pull and sandbag carry are where Hyrox races are lost. Athletes without hip drive and loaded carry strength collapse in form under fatigue. This is the most race-specific strength gap to address.
Running economy strength
Single-leg power, hip stability
Single-leg RDL, step-up, hip thrust, calf raises, ankle stability
 Most Hyrox athletes come from running backgrounds with underdeveloped single-leg strength and hip stability — the exact traits that deteriorate pace over 8km of running under load. This track improves running economy without adding run volume.
Station-specific strength
Wall ball, SkiErg, rowing, burpees
Goblet squat, pull-over, rowing-specific lat work, burpee-specific conditioning
 Targets the exact movements in the race that aren't addressed by general strength training. SkiErg demands lat and core power. Rowing demands posterior chain. Wall ball demands front squat strength under fatigue. Each station has a strength prerequisite.
Strength endurance
Aerobic strength, fatigue resistance
EMOMs, moderate-load complexes, barbell cycling, timed sets
 Hyrox punishes athletes who are strong in isolation but weak under accumulated fatigue. This track trains the ability to maintain output on station 6, 7, 8 when the legs are already compromised from running.
ATHX 6-week cycles · 3–4 days/week
ATHX Explosive power
Concentric rate of force development
Power clean, hang power snatch, box jump, jump squat, trap bar DL
 ATHX demands explosive output across many modalities — more so than Hyrox. Athletes need to develop rate of force development without full Olympic lifting technical demands. This track bridges that gap with power-focused variations.
Posterior chain & hinge
Deadlift patterns, hip extension
Conventional DL, Romanian DL, good morning, glute-ham raise
 ATHX athletes often have CF backgrounds with underdeveloped hinge patterns — back squats but no deadlifts. Posterior chain strength directly translates to sprinting, jumping, and sled-based movements that appear in ATHX programming.
Upper body power
Horizontal and vertical push/pull
Floor press, DB bench, weighted dips, landmine press, pendlay row
 ATHX includes more upper body loading than Hyrox. Athletes need both horizontal and vertical push/pull strength. This track fills the gap between gymnastic volume and actual loaded upper body strength development.
Competition prep
Full-body strength with conditioning
Complex-style programming, timed circuits, sport-specific loading
 A peaking sub-track for athletes 6–8 weeks out from an ATHX event. Higher intensity, lower volume, sport-specific loading patterns. Designed to be run after completing one of the other ATHX tracks.
